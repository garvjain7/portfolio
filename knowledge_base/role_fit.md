# Role Fit

## Strongest Fit

### Backend Engineer

This is where Garv's demonstrated depth is clearest. Across ScanVista, DataInsights.ai, CodeAlive, and ComplySense he has designed multi-tenant schemas, implemented JWT auth with refresh token rotation, built caching layers with Redis, written raw parameterized SQL across 19–28 table schemas, and structured APIs with ownership-checked queries and RBAC. He has done this across both Node.js/Express and FastAPI/Python stacks.

The depth is not just feature implementation — he has reasoned about data isolation, auth security, schema evolution, and API contract design at an architectural level across multiple projects simultaneously.

### AI/Data Engineer (Applied)

Garv has built RAG pipelines from scratch, integrated local LLMs via Ollama and external APIs via OpenAI, designed vector retrieval systems with FAISS, and built the compute-first architecture in DataInsights.ai where LLMs operate only on pre-computed summaries rather than raw data. He holds a Databricks Certified Data Engineer Associate certification.

The qualifier is "applied" — this is AI engineering in the sense of building products and systems that use AI, not research-level ML. He has no production PyTorch or model training experience and does not claim otherwise.

### Technical Lead on Small Teams

Garv has functioned as technical lead on two concurrent projects — ScanVista at SparkIIT and ComplySense at STPI — while still an undergraduate. In both cases he was responsible for architecture decisions, implementation direction, and team coordination. The evidence for this is in the projects themselves, not just the title.

## Partial Fit

### Full Stack Engineer

Garv builds frontend competently — React, Next.js, Three.js, Framer Motion — and has delivered complete full-stack products. The fit is partial because his depth and genuine interest are on the backend side. A full-stack role that expects equal depth on both sides, or one that is primarily frontend with some backend, is not the strongest use of his profile.

### DevOps / Infrastructure Engineer

RHCSA and RHCE certifications give Garv genuine Linux and system administration depth that most developers at his level do not have. He understands process management, storage, networking, and enterprise RHEL environments. However, his production DevOps experience is limited — Docker depth is surface level, and he has not managed production infrastructure beyond deployment on Render and Vercel. A DevOps role would require significant ramp-up in areas he has not yet worked in.

## Not a Fit

### Frontend-Only Engineer

Garv builds frontend when projects require it. He does not gravitate toward frontend problems, does not have depth in frontend performance, accessibility, or design systems, and has explicitly stated this is not the direction he wants to move.

### ML Research Engineer / Data Scientist

Garv has theoretical ML knowledge and understands core concepts well enough to apply them in product contexts. He has no production experience with PyTorch, no model training work, and approximately 30% practical depth in ML overall. Roles requiring production ML engineering or research-level model development are not a fit at this stage.

### Pure Integration / Maintenance Engineering

Roles where the primary work is integrating third-party services, maintaining existing codebases, or implementing predefined specifications without architectural involvement are misaligned with how Garv works best. His strongest output comes when he has genuine ownership over architectural decisions.
