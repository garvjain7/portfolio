# Career Goals

## Target Roles

Garv is targeting backend engineering, full-stack engineering with backend depth, or AI/data engineering roles. The common thread is work that involves designing and building systems — not maintaining existing ones, not primarily UI work, and not roles where engineering is secondary to process.

Day-to-day, he wants to be making architectural decisions, building things that handle real complexity, and working on problems where the engineering itself is the interesting part. His project history — multi-tenant SaaS, real-time collaboration, AI pipelines, compliance platforms — reflects the kind of depth he wants to continue working at.

He is also open to technical lead roles on small teams where he has demonstrated he can operate effectively, having done so on ScanVista and ComplySense simultaneously during his undergraduate years.

## Preferred Company Type

Garv gravitates toward product companies and early-stage startups over service companies. The reason is consistent with how he learns — he builds better when he owns a problem end to end and can see how engineering decisions affect the product. Service environments where work is scoped to client tickets and implementation is separated from architecture are less aligned with how he works.

Research-adjacent roles in applied AI are also of genuine interest, particularly those involving RAG systems, data pipelines, or LLM applications — areas where his practical work already has depth.

## Problem Domains

The domains Garv is most drawn to:

- **Backend infrastructure and data systems** — systems that handle scale, multi-tenancy, complex access patterns, or high-volume data flow
- **AI applications** — practical AI integration, RAG systems, LLM-powered products; not research-level ML, but engineering AI into real products
- **Developer tools and platforms** — tools that other engineers use, which combine product thinking with technical depth
- **Compliance, governance, or enterprise SaaS** — exposure through ComplySense has given him genuine understanding of this domain and its engineering complexity

## Realistic 3–5 Year View

By the time Garv graduates in 2027, he will have three internships, two Red Hat certifications, a Databricks certification, GATE qualification, and six or more production-level projects across backend systems, AI integration, and real-time systems. That is an unusual profile for a fresh graduate.

In the three to five years following graduation, the realistic path is a backend or full-stack engineering role at a product company, deepening system design and distributed systems knowledge through production experience at scale, and progressively taking on architectural ownership rather than just implementation.

Building something independently — a product or tool — is also a genuine medium-term interest, consistent with the pattern of how he has operated throughout his degree.

## What He Is Not Looking For

Garv is not targeting frontend-only roles, UI/UX positions, or roles where engineering is largely integration work with minimal architectural involvement. He is also not interested in roles that require pure ML research depth — his ML knowledge is theoretical and he is honest about that gap. Roles that would ask him to represent production ML engineering depth he does not yet have are not a fit.
