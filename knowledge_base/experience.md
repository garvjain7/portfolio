# Experience

## SparkIIT — Backend Engineer and Technical Lead (Remote)

**Duration:** ~3 months (approx. 3–4 months in practice)
**Type:** Remote internship, team-based

At SparkIIT, Garv worked as a backend engineer and team lead across two concurrent projects. This was his first professional environment involving team coordination, requirement gathering, and delivery under real deadlines.

**DataInsights.ai** — Built for a client company. Garv contributed to backend architecture, data ingestion pipelines, and AI integration. Internal implementation details are confidential, but the project involved the same core stack and architectural patterns reflected in his personal version of DataInsights.ai: multi-tenant data handling, local LLM integration with Ollama, and progressive data report generation. Stack included Node.js, FastAPI, Python, PostgreSQL, MongoDB, and local LLM optimization.

**ScanVista** — Developed under this internship and fully owned by Garv. A QR-to-3D product visualization platform with AR support. Garv led the full architecture and implementation including React Three Fiber 3D viewer, AR via model-viewer, Node.js + PostgreSQL backend, Supabase Storage, Redis, and a Python FastAPI AI microservice. Also involved voice recognition integration and Three.js canvas work. Full project details in `projects/scanvista.md`.

The SparkIIT role exposed Garv to the difference between building alone and building in a team — managing shared understanding of architecture, coordinating across parallel workstreams, and maintaining consistency in codebases that multiple people touch simultaneously.

## STPI Jaipur — First Internship (Solo)

**Duration:** 45 days
**Type:** On-site internship, independent

Garv built ApnaEV as a solo project over 45 days at STPI Jaipur. The platform is an electric vehicle charging station discovery tool designed for Jaipur — helping users locate charging stations, explore station details, plan routes, and build confidence in EV travel.

This was Garv's first complete end-to-end product built in a professional context. It covered location-based systems, geospatial data handling, mapping integration via Leaflet, backend API design, and external data source integration — areas he had not worked in before.

From an engineering standpoint, ApnaEV was significant not for its complexity but for what it taught: how to take a real-world problem, model it into a working product, and deliver it independently under a fixed timeline. It was the first time Garv experienced the full product lifecycle — from understanding a problem to shipping something usable — without any team support.

Full project details in `projects/apnaev.md`.

## STPI Jaipur — Second Internship (Technical Lead, Ongoing)

**Duration:** Ongoing
**Type:** On-site internship, team-based

Garv is currently working at STPI Jaipur as the technical lead on ComplySense — a GRC (Governance, Risk, and Compliance) SaaS platform designed for Indian universities. He is the sole person responsible for architectural decisions, implementation direction, and technical execution across the team.

ComplySense maps six major Indian regulatory and international compliance frameworks — DPDP Act 2023, IT Act, ISO 27001:2022, ISO 27701, NIST CSF 2.0, and NIST SP 800-53 — into a unified control library. The platform has a 28-table PostgreSQL schema, nine RBAC roles, async SQLAlchemy ORM, and a MongoDB-backed control library. Local Ollama LLMs are used exclusively — no external API calls — for data sovereignty.

This internship represents Garv's most complex professional engagement to date, requiring simultaneous ownership of architecture, team direction, and delivery.

Full project details in `projects/complysense.md`.

## What Internships Added Beyond Personal Projects

Personal projects gave Garv architectural depth and technical range. Internships added a different layer:

- **Consistency under external deadlines** — shipping features on a schedule set by someone other than himself, with real accountability
- **Requirement translation** — taking vague client or product requirements and converting them into concrete engineering decisions
- **Team coordination** — managing shared understanding of a codebase, aligning on implementation approaches, and keeping parallel work from conflicting
- **Professional context** — understanding the difference between code that works and code that a team can maintain and build on

The combination of solo internship work (ApnaEV) and team lead roles (SparkIIT, ComplySense) means Garv has experienced both ends of professional engineering responsibility at an early stage.
